Background

I've lived in Las Vegas with my wife for more than 20 years. Following college, we made the decision to escape the Utah snow and ended up in the sweltering Nevada desert. In addition to the fact that there is no state tax, Las Vegas has a ton of fantastic restaurants, a ton of things to do, and that snow ❄ is less than an hour away during winter, among other things.

Like many Las Vegas residents, I worked for one of the properties on the Strip as a Financial Planning Analyst for about a year and decided the hospitality industry in Las Vegas wasn't for me. Following my casion career, I was hired as a merchandising analyst at Zappos.com, an online softgoods retailer. Zappos is a fantastic company that values its customers, vendors, and employees. During my 12 years at Zappos, I began as a merchandising analyst and quickly advanced to merchandising planner. For about four years, I worked in merchandise planning, then as an inventory analyst, and finally as a business analyst. Unfortunately, I was laid off in February as a consequence of the economic downturn.

Why Web Developer

I had some time with the ladioff to think about my next step in my career. When I first started college, I considered studying Computer Science and even took a few programming classes, but in the end, I chose a different path. So far, I've spent the majority of my career analyzing things or businesses; the layoff provided me with an opportunity to change careers. I wanted to build something, so I thought about software development. Also, my children are interested in coding, so I thought it might be a fun family activity we could do, and if I'm in the field, I could provide them with some guidance as they pursue the software development path.

Hobbies

I am extremely enthusiastic about two things: sports and food. Most of the sports I enjoyed, but soccer was my favorite. I enjoy watching, playing, and coaching soccer. In the past, I coached youth and club soccer. I've been coaching at the high school level for the past two years. I work as an assistant coach at Clark High School, assisting the head coach on varsity and overseeing the junior varsity. High school sports are a little different because school administrators believe that they are an extension of the classroom. As coaches, we have the responsibility to teach high school students about things other than sports, such as respect, communication, and work ethic. I enjoyed working with high school students and assisting them in preparing for life after high school.

Food is another one of my interests. I enjoy trying food from various cultures. I enjoy going out to new restaurants with my wife, as well as cooking and experimenting with new recipes. I've recently become obsessed with making desserts.
